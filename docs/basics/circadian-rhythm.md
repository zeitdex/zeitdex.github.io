# Circadian rhythm & zeitgebers

The **circadian rhythm** is the roughly 24-hour internal cycle that regulates sleep, alertness, body temperature, and hormone release. It is generated by a master clock in the brain (the [suprachiasmatic nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suprachiasmatic_nucleus)) and kept aligned to the day by external time cues.

## Zeitgebers

A **zeitgeber** ("time-giver") is an external cue that entrains the circadian clock to the 24-hour day:

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

- :material-white-balance-sunny:{ .lg .middle } &nbsp; **Light** — the most powerful zeitgeber, especially bright morning light.
- :material-silverware-fork-knife:{ .lg .middle } &nbsp; **Meals** — when you eat across the day.
- :material-run:{ .lg .middle } &nbsp; **Activity** — exercise and physical activity.
- :material-account-group:{ .lg .middle } &nbsp; **Social routine** — work, school, and contact with others.

</div>

In many circadian rhythm disorders the clock either runs at a period different from 24 hours, or fails to stay entrained to its zeitgebers.
